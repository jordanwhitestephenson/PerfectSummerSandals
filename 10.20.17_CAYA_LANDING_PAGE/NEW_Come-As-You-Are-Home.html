COME AS YOU ARE: ARTISTIC INTERPRETATIONS

We asked our fans to tell us how their experiences have shaped what Come As You Are means to them. Then we shared some of our favorite responses with artists and asked them to express these CAYA stories in their chosen medium. These are the results!

  • To @danaikadzere, #ComeAsYouAre means not trying to be normal. She's a first generation immigrant and self-proclaimed "HUGE nerd" who embraces her multiculturalism and sunny disposition in all that she does.
    —
    art by @pavlovvisuals
  • Jennifer Bulcock shared her #ComeAsYouAre story on Facebook and we worked with talented #artist @mdemilan to bring it to life. Jennifer says #ComeAsYouAre means working hard and doing one's best wherever/whoever you are.
    —
    art by @mdemilan
  • One of our fans, @afitandfabulousmichele, was brave enough to share her struggle and remind us that everyone has a struggle of one kind or another. A #ComeAsYouAre contribution vividly rendered by artist @juliesongink.
    —
    art by @juliesongink
  • Welcoming one-of-a-kinds of all different kinds is an important part of #ComeAsYouAre, as @kendralydick so eloquently described. We passed this on to artist @mdemilan and instantly fell in love with the result.
    —
    art by @mdemilan
  • Jo Ann Costa O'Shaughnessy responded to #ComeAsYouAre with keen insight: Who needs perfection when you aren't afraid of trying new things? Artist @bodiljane illustrates the message in lovely detail.
    —
    art by @bodiljane
  • It's unfortunate that sometimes there are consequences to simply being good. Michelle Sabre Prieto's story of how to #ComeAsYouAre. Artist @pavlovvisuals gets to the heart of it all!
    —
    art by @pavlovvisuals
